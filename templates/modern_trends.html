Interesting Trends

A few interesting trends from this map were identified

  • The maps partitionings are almost identical from one data set to the next across eras, especially in the heart of the former Confederacy, showing how attitudes regarding race and equality haven't changed that significantly since the conclusion of the Civil War
  • The percentage of African-Americans who have been disenfranchised due to felon disenfranchisement laws is highest in those states which were most invested in the original institution of slavery. These laws disproportionately affect African-Americans, illustrating how sometimes even neutral-sounding laws can be used to perpetuate institutional racism. As Lee Atwater admitted in his infamous 1981 interview on the Southern Strategy, though blatant racism "hurts you" in modern-day American, coded language and "abstract" policies that result in "blacks get[ting] hurt worse than whites" allowed Republican politicians to appeal to the same racist white voters that voted Democratic in the Jim Crow era
  • All the Confederacy had to do to win the Civil War was not lose to a foreign invader, and although its armies lost on the battlefield, charts like this show that the South was ultimately able to preserve its racist institutions and ways of life
